Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

ACREOL Global (African Center for Research on End of Life) is a non-governmental organization dedicated to improving health outcomes through evidence-based policy influence, education, and innovation. We focus on public health issues, including palliative care, and strive to make research more accessible and impactful.

Our core areas of expertise include:
● Health research and policy advocacy on palliative care;
● Evidence-based decision-making in public health;
● Capacity-building through training and education;
● Innovation in healthcare solutions, including democratizing palliative care

We conduct rigorous studies on pressing health issues, analyze data to inform policies, and publish research findings to drive meaningful change. Our work ensures that healthcare interventions are guided by scientific evidence.

We welcome collaborations with researchers, policymakers, healthcare professionals, NGOs, government institutions, and academic bodies. We believe in multi-sectoral partnerships to advance health research and advocacy.

Yes! We offer mentorship programs and internship opportunities for students and early-career professionals interested in health research, advocacy, and policy development. These programs help build the next generation of health leaders.

We focus on making palliative and end-of-life care more accessible and well-integrated into healthcare systems. Through research, training, and advocacy, we aim to ensure that people facing life-limiting illnesses receive dignified and compassionate care.

You can support us by:
● Partnering with us on research projects
● Participating in our mentorship and training programs
● Spreading awareness about our work
● Donating to help expand our initiatives

You can visit our website at www.acreol.org for the latest updates on our projects, publications, and opportunities to get involved. You can also follow us on our social media channels for more insights into our work.
